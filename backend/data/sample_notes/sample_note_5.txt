Subject: 22yo male, soccer injury
Date: 01/15/2026

History:
Patient twisted right ankle while playing soccer yesterday. Landed on opponent's foot.
Reports immediate pain on outer aspect of ankle. Able to limp off field but unable to bear full weight today.
Swelling noted immediately. Used ice and elevation overnight.

Exam:
Ankle: Significant ecchymosis and edema over lateral malleolus.
Tenderness: +++ over ATFL and CFL ligaments. No tenderness over medial malleolus.
Ottawa Ankle Rules: Negative for bony tenderness at posterior edge of distal 6cm of fibula/tibia.
Range of Motion: Limited inversion/eversion secondary to pain.
Neurovascular: Distal pulses 2+ and symmetric. Sensation intact.
X-ray Right Ankle: No fracture or dislocation. Soft tissue swelling noted.

Diagnosis:
1. Right Ankle Sprain - Grade 2 Lateral Ligament Complex
2. Gait abnormality

Treatment Plan:
1. RICE protocol (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation)
2. Air splint/brace to be worn when walking
3. Crutches for 3 days, weight bear as tolerated
4. NSAIDs (Naproxen 500mg BID) for 5 days with food
5. Physical Therapy referral for strengthening once acute pain subsides
6. Follow up if no improvement in 2 weeks
