Patient: 5-year-old male
CC: Ear pain and fever
HPI: Mother reports patient woke up crying at 2 AM complaining of right ear pain. 
Had runny nose and cough for 3 days prior. Fevers up to 102°F at home, treated with Tylenol.
Decreased appetite, good fluid intake. No vomiting or diarrhea.
PMH: Recurrent otitis media (3 episodes in last year). No surgeries.
Allergies: Penicillin (hives)

Physical Exam:
VS: Temp 101.5°F, HR 110, RR 24, SpO2 99%
Gen: Alert, fussy but consolable
HEENT: 
- Right TM: Erythematous, bulging, poor mobility, purulent effusion visible
- Left TM: Clear, good light reflex
- Oropharynx: Mild erythema, no exudates
- Neck: Small anterior cervical lymph nodes palpable
Lungs: Clear to auscultation bilaterally

Assessment:
1. Acute Otitis Media, Right Suppurative - recurrent
2. Upper Respiratory Infection
3. Penicillin Allergy

Plan:
1. Cefdinir 14mg/kg/day PO x 10 days (due to PCN allergy)
2. Acetaminophen/Ibuprofen for pain/fever control
3. Encourage hydration
4. Return precautions: persistent fever > 48hrs, lethargy, worsening pain
5. Referral to ENT if one more episode within 6 months
